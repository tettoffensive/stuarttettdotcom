Channel began as an app that allowed users to edit videos together on their phone and post them into "channels" with other related videos. One of the issues early on in development was the experience of the content creator.

When a user posts a video, it is processed on the device, and uploaded to the server. From there it is encoded on the backend. Even as we improved our encoding time, it was still not instantaneous.

Users were confused when they posted a video, but it didn't show up in the channel right away.

It didn't feel like the post was successful.

Part of my role as developer was to solve user experience issues like this that involved technical solutions.

I came up with a solution of keeping a cached copy of the video and inserting it at the start of the channel feed.

The video would then be removed once it came down from the server after encoding.

From the users perspective, when they posted a new video, the app went straight to the channel they posted in and started playing their video. While there may be a delay in the rest of the world seeing the new video, the user seeing their own video right away gives them that validation that their content is out there.
