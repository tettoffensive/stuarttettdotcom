I've included my demo reel here to show my background. I decided to leave the film industry in 2013 because of the state of the industry. Due to lower box office numbers and DVD sales, as well as tax incentives in foreign countries, most of the studios in the U.S. were closing.

I was usually assigned a "shot" or maybe a group of related "shots" at a time. My task was to come up with what the rendering and animation of a particular effect like fire would look like in a shot. We would often scour YouTube for reference or shoot our own.

After an initial pass, I would present to the Head of Effects and would iterate on that feedback. Eventually the Visual Effects Supervisor and Director(s) would get involved until it was final.

At DreamWorks, effects artists integrate their own effects (composite) with the renders from the lighting department.

My student reel shows how I used art and programming while in graduate school at the VizLab. This is what lead to me being hired at DreamWorks.
