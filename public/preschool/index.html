New parents in cities like San Francisco often hear they should get on preschool wait lists before their child is even born.

It's a daunting task to research and compare all the different options.

How can we help busy parents find schools that will work for them?

I consulted with new parents to find out what criteria they look for in a preschool. It was a long list!

I needed to narrow down what what most important and/or condense all the information, yet allow parents access to all the important details.

I began to explore presenting a collection of schools that would work logistically first. Working parents need to have a school in a convenient location with convenient hours.

How can we help working parents find a preschool that works for pick up and drop off?

My solution asks users for work and home addresses to find schools along the route.

I also included a caretaker address, since many working parents have additional care.

I later discovered one preschool's website to have a similar feature. The route assumed I drove the two miles to downtown and failed to give me good school choices.

This triggered me to add options for Bike/Car/Transit. In the future, I would consider a way to let people manually edit their route to work.

How can we present the collection of preschools in an easily digestible way?

I looked to sites like Yelp and Airbnb to see how they presented information. Icons could condense much of the criteria onto a collection of cards without too much clutter.

Navigating a large map is cumbersome. The user cares: "Is it near my home or work?", "How long will it take to get there?"

I placed a map on each card to help the user understand exactly where the school is.

Parents might also like to visualize what kind of environment the school is in. I tried a version using Google street view images.

Filtering results (Ã  la Yelp) poses an issue as preschools aren't as plentiful as restaurants.

Dimming, instead of removing non-matches, still allows users to explore all options.

What's the best way to let the user dive deep into the details?

The most important details can be found by clicking on a result. This view has lots of copy explaining details about the preschool as well as the direct link to apply.

Every preschool has a different process, an opportunity to unify the application process through this platform in the future.

How can we better help users compare schools?

Online shopping sites often have comparison features which are very useful, but get quickly out of control.

I explored different ways to organize the similarities and differences between preschools.

Using a Venn style diagram is a much simpler presentation than a table. But limits to comparing 2 or 3 schools at once.

A table is more familiar looking and can handle more comparisons, but it's more challenging to identify similarities.

Presenting the table and allowing users to select 2 to 3 to compare via Venn helps give them more insight.

Some other things I'd like to explore for this project are:

  • Asking users for reviews/ratings
  • Keeping track of applications/waitlists
  • Allowing partners to record/share notes about schools
