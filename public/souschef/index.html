SousChef was originally an idea formed my me and my co-founder to develop an app that utilized the iPhone's computer vision capabilities to recognize foods.

Yes, it is similar to the Not a Hot Dog app from HBO's Silicon Valley ;)

After some early exploration of mocking up some screens, I realized that the core problem is meal-planning. And that it's best served as a recipe-first app. Not a camera-first app.

Users want to know what they can make with the ingredients that they have. But they also want to stop throwing out food that goes bad because they don't use it.

I went back to the drawing board and came up with some user personas. I chose 3 to work with.

The main difference between the personas is how they want the suggested recipes sorted and filtered. They all prioritize different things in the meals they want to cook.

I then explored a few variations for each of the main viewsâ€” the recipes, the ingredients pantry, and the recipe filtering.

I decided that the home base of the app will be the recipes view. Once a recipe is chosen the user can press a button to "start" the recipe.

From the tab bar the user can navigate to the pantry. Here they can manually add/remove ingredients as well as search for recipes based on specific ingredients. In the future they may add ingredients by camera, smart fridge, or voice command.

The filter menu is opened by touching the screen and dragging the finger down. Originally,I had the x buttons on the filters removing the item from the list. I realized that this would require another interface to add a filter back to the list. So instead I changed it to just gray out the item and place it at the end. A tap on the + button brings it right back.
