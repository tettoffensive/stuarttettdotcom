When I joined Binary Thumb, the Grid app had released its first version into the App Store. The app allowed users to collaborate on spreadsheets full of video, images, and text in real-time. It was a solid MVP, but their were some much needed improvements in the overall experience.

The spreadsheet was an infinite canvas of tiles. There were no limitations on the number of images, and how many tiles each images takes up.

This was causing performance issues.

Grid may be a spreadsheet, but it is meant to showcase multimedia in a beautiful way.

The engineering team had optimized the loading/unloading of tiles dynamically, but the animation still wasn't buttery smooth.

I made a prototype of the home view in OpenGL, and the performance looked promising.

We converted the spreadsheet canvas to OpenGL and stress tested it. We were now getting a consistent 60fps. It felt so much more responsive in the hands of the user.

While the new user experience was superior, the conversion had some major engineering challenges. It was costly to rebuild standard UI components.

Another experience issue came with the introduction of Backpack. This let the user pick photos and videos to add to the Grid. But it took up so much screen real estate.

I came up with a solution of quickly fading out the Backpack when the user dragged an element out to be placed on the grid. This way the user could see more of the canvas while placing, but still have a large enough Backpack to easily explore and choose.
