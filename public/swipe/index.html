When I joined Swipe Labs, the Swipe app was already gaining traction. But retention was one of the issues hindering its growth.

One of the strategies was to bring on social media influencers onto the platform to post higher quality content.

Many of those influencers had profressional level photographs that they were posting on the Swipe.

But due to Swipe's full-screen vertical only format, many of these landscape and square photographs were getting cutoff at the edges.

We didn't want to lose Swipe's full-screen vertical format that helped make our app unique.

We needed a way for users to see the whole image that was easy discover. This ruled out any special gestures. And we didn't want to add any new UI elements.

I decided to try an idea where the user could tilt the phone from side to side to pan across the image.

I put together a prototype and showed it to the team. We ended up shipping it to production and it was a hit! It solved the problem and was a fun feature for users.
