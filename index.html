I’ve been an effects artist since graduating from school with degrees in Computer Science and Art. I spent the first 6 years of my career working at DreamWorks Animation (DWA) on full CG animated features and Prime Focus working on CG effects for live-action films. It was a wonderful experience, but I’m now looking for opportunities outside the film industry, while still using the strong visual and analytical problem-solving skills. With these I have written lines of code to produce a beautiful image from a blank canvas. I have the skill-set to analyze the design of both software and an image. I am looking for opportunities that will allow me to exercise these opposite but intertwined areas.

At DWA, my responsibilities as an effects artist included: effects look development, particle, volume and rigid-body simulation, keyframe animation, pipeline programming, scripting, packaging effects systems, cg lighting of volumes and particles, and compositing. I am able to do tasks in many areas of the pipeline and often had to develop new pipeline tools to accomplish my work efficiently. In addition, I have been the representative to Research & Development Pipeline team for the Effects department at DWA. As the representative, I worked with the team to make sure the pipeline software is meeting the needs of our department and helped design some of the tools. I have also been involved as a mentor and instructor in DWA Effects Challenge 6-month program (from which I started in out of school).

Some of the effects I’ve done include: explosions, fire, smoke, dust, rigid-body destruction, turbulent river water, lasers, sparks, liquid metal. Most of these were done using Maya and Houdini, as well as proprietary software (some of which I have coded to help the workflow for myself and other artists).
